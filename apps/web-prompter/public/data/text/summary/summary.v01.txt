This article examines the debate over the origin of life, drawing on insights from scientists and theorists who have studied the difficult process of creating life in the lab. It also covers current research into the use of molecules and graphene to advance medicine, environmental issues, automotive industry, and aviation technology. The conclusion is that life has yet to be accurately defined and dead cells have yet to be revived, and there is an inaccurate belief that scientists have already created living organisms.
